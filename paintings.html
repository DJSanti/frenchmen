Allie and Jake, the duo behind Anchored in Flight, have made it their mission to provide New Orleans with visionary art, apparel, and more. Angel was born in Honduras, but later moved to New Orleans to pursue a degree in the arts. Angelle lives for a live that is full of color. She wants to share her art and spread her NOLA love through homes across the world. She works with acrylic on canvas, using molding paste styles to create different textures, and also does abstract pieces and custom pet portraits.

Angelo Olegna grew up in St. Louis and developed into an artist of architecture. He specialized in highly detailed individual buildings and dense cityscapes. Now he wants to represent our local architecture through pen drawings on upcycled beadboard.

Ben Markus started working as an artisan in 1994 while studying Cultural Anthropology, Philosophy, and Literature at the University of Leripzig/Germany. Brandy Dufrene is a local artist whose work depicts the natural in an original way: by capturing the imperfections, gnarls, and beautiful decay that make life interesting. Natural and unusual pigments, reclaimed wood, and found items make their way into her work.

Heather's artwork involves using a reverse painting process called Pareidolia Matrixing, where the paint is removed to leave behind a stained canvas. The resulting artwork is very abstract, almost like a Rorschach Ink Blot, where the mind is left to see shapes where there are none.

Heathergreyart can be found on her website, Etsy, and Facebook.

José Torres-Tama studied traditional drawing at the Arts Student League in New York City before finding his way down south. Some local icons that have graced his works include Marie Laveau and Louis Armstrong.
